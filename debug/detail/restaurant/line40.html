    This is my favorite place I've gone to happy hour in FiDi! Happy hour here lasts till 8 pm! All the bites are $5 and they are so delicious that you know you are getting a deal! Drinks vary in prices from $5-$7.

    Friendly customer service as well!

    I had the sake bomb. It was the right amount of strong and the right mild beer taste for me.

    We ordered the ribs and wings. I was most impressed by the ribs! The sauce was spicy and sweet. The right mix between Japanese and American that Ramen Bar is going for.

    The wings were ok--I think there could have been more meat on wings we were given and less oil too. The sweet/savory taste was pretty good! These were more sweet than savory.

    I also had a taste of my friend's boozy boba. This was a delight to taste. It has a very strong bourbon taste, which was unexpected. The tapioca balls in it are the right amount of chewy and sweet. This makes me think the milk tea here will be worth trying.

    Overall, I would definitely come back for the happy hour. I am also excited to return and try their ramen.

    Came here for lunch yesterday and here's the lowdown:

    -Location: Located near the Embarcadero center in FiDi, this place is pretty conveniently located if you work downtown, or are here as a tourist. The courtyard outside is very beautiful with giant sculptures that double as seating for nice days.

    -Ambiance: Very trendy and hipster. The restaurant is very clean with a modern touch. Menus are located on giant wooden plaques behind the counter, as well as in paper format near the line. Plenty of seating inside to choose from.

    -Service: Very friendly cashiers and waiters/busboys. They make sure to stop by our table often to refill waters and ask if we needed anything. As soon as we left the table, they were very quick to clean the table down and align the seating.

    -Food: Ordered the spicy Tonkotsu w/ pork, while the rest of the team ordered the same thing but in chicken. Presentation was very well done and definitely photogenic. The noodles were very good and was quite the burst of flavor and a pleasure to consume. The pork slices were also good, while the broth, though good, was a bit too oily for my taste. And the soft boiled eggs were just ok.

    -Price: Pretty average and expected for a ramen place. Price was $14 for the tonkotsu bowl, which is pretty average for the Bay Area.

    Being a sucker for good service, I definitely wouldn't mind coming back here again if I'm in the area!
